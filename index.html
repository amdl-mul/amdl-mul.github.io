Course Description

This course offers a graduate-level exploration of the mathematical and algorithmic foundations of machine learning, with an emphasis on deep neural networks. Students will study optimization theory, generalization bounds, and architecture design before advancing to contemporary topics such as transformers, diffusion models, and reinforcement learning from human feedback.

Core Topics

ML Foundations

Bias-variance tradeoff, hypothesis space, evaluation metrics, MLE, MAP estimation, probability distributions, KL divergence & information-theoretic measures

Neural Networks & Optimization

FFNs, expressivity, activation functions, SGD, momentum, Nesterov, Adam, LR scheduling, gradient clipping

Architectures

CNNs, RNNs, LSTMs, Attention, Transformers, SSMs, xLSTMs

Generative Models

VAEs, GANs, Diffusion Models, LLMs
